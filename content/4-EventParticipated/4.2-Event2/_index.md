### Key Highlights

#### From Real-Life Stories to Corporate Culture at a Multinational Corporation

The presentation “What Does a Data Analytics Engineer Actually Do?” provides a realistic and practical view of the role. It shows how the daily work of a Data Analytics Engineer varies greatly depending on the industry (domain) and company structure. Through real examples from Kamereo and Colgate-Palmolive, the speaker illustrated typical responsibilities such as building reports/dashboards, analyzing business metrics, optimizing operations with IoT data, and solving cross-functional problems.
The talk also emphasized essential soft & hard skills: critical thinking, storytelling with data, problem-solving, and effective communication. It introduced a personal career development model with 5 stages (Follower → Learner → Problem Solver → System Thinker → Super Star) and shared valuable insights on corporate culture at multinational companies (MNCs), recruitment processes, and global standards.

#### What does a DevOps Engineer really do?

The presentation “What does a DevOps Engineer really do?” clarifies the common misconceptions about the DevOps role. While many people think DevOps is just about writing CI/CD pipelines, managing Docker/Kubernetes, or being the person who deploys code and fixes production issues at midnight, the reality is much broader and highly context-dependent.
The speaker explained that the actual scope of a DevOps Engineer varies based on company size, team structure, project complexity, and infrastructure maturity. In real life, DevOps work includes on-call rotation, incident handling, troubleshooting, cost optimization, environment support, and enabling developers to ship code reliably and quickly.

#### From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner
The presentation “From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner” shares an inspiring personal and community journey. It outlines an 8-step roadmap from a student’s initial curiosity about cloud technology to becoming an official AWS Partner. The key stages include: sparking curiosity, starting the first cloud journey, joining workshops & community, hands-on labs, applying knowledge through school projects, building a strong portfolio, collaborating with AWS Partner, and finally giving back to the community.
The speaker also introduced the Student Community Day program with clear benefits, badges, AWS credits, certification vouchers, and swag points to motivate and support students throughout their learning journey.

#### A scalable URL shortening service on AWS
The presentation “A scalable URL shortening service on AWS” demonstrates how to design and build a production-grade URL shortener using AWS services. It starts with a simple flow (User → Frontend → Backend → Database) and then evolves into a robust, scalable architecture.
Key components include:

- Frontend: Amazon CloudFront + AWS WAF + Amplify
- Backend: Spring Boot on Amazon ECS
- Key Generation: Pre-generating short codes using ECS + Redis (ElastiCache)
- Storage: Amazon DynamoDB

The architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, defense at the edge, pre-computation, and cache-first strategy to achieve high performance and scalability. 

### Key Takeaways

#### From Real-Life Stories to Corporate Culture at a Multinational Corporation
- A Data Analytics Engineer is not just a “report maker” but a business problem solver who turns data into actionable insights.
- Technical skills must be combined with strong critical thinking, communication, and business understanding.
- Career growth should focus on mindset evolution rather than just job titles.
#### What does a DevOps Engineer really do? 
- DevOps is not a specific tool or job title — it is a culture and way of working that bridges Development and Operations.
- Fundamentals (Linux, networking, Git, containers, observability) are more important than chasing trendy tools.
- A good DevOps Engineer must be curious, think in systems, automate repetitive work, communicate clearly, and continuously learn.
- Success comes from understanding “why” before “how” and focusing on making the entire team more effective, not being a hero.

#### A scalable URL shortening service on AWS
- A simple URL shortener can become complex when scalability, security, and performance are required.
- Leveraging AWS managed services (CloudFront, WAF, ECS, ElastiCache, DynamoDB) helps build secure, scalable systems efficiently.
- Good architecture focuses on separation of read/write paths and pushing security & caching closer to the user.
